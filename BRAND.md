# LATE CULTURE — Brand Voice & Tone

> Write like a reference authority. Not a content creator.

## Positioning Statement

Late Culture is not a blog. It is not a travel magazine. It is a structured, curated, long-term hospitality reference infrastructure. Write accordingly.

## The Six Principles

| Principle | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Calm** | No exclamation marks. No urgency. Quiet authority. |
| **Precise** | Every word earns its place. Adjectives must be functional, not decorative. |
| **Observational** | Describe what is there, not what it feels like. |
| **Confident** | No hedging. No 'seems' or 'appears'. State, don't suggest. |
| **Institutional** | Write like a reference, not a recommendation. |
| **Analytical** | Position venues structurally. Context over enthusiasm. |

## Good Examples

- Bisou operates a rotating weekly menu. Forty covers. Service is precise.
- Sorn sources from 14 southern Thai provinces. Twenty-two covers.
- The Siam. Bill Bensley. Thirty-nine rooms. Art-deco. Muay Thai ring on site.

## Banned Words

Never use: hidden gem, instagrammable, vibes, must-visit, trendy, coolest, amazing, stunning, iconic, world-class, cutting-edge, game-changer, foodie, culinary journey, passion, love

## Selection Language

- Inclusion = endorsement. No qualifying statements needed.
- No rankings. Order is alphabetical or by district.
- No scores. No stars, no ratings, no 8.5/10.
- Exclusion is the work. What we leave out matters.
